# Learning by doing

I'd like to think that I'll be always be tinkering with tech and see myself as a [lifelong learner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lifelong_learning).

One thing that I haven't done too much however is the whole 'learn in public' thing. It takes a great deal of discipline and vulnerability. Let's see how this goes! My plan is to write and post something every Thursday. Length and content will likely vary but I plan to keep it within the context below.

### Learn what?

As someone who got into tech around the time of the Millenium Bug (some folks in the workplace were not born!) my interests have evolved over time:

- **Software Engineering & Coding:** - I first learnt to code in high school (some BASIC then thrown into the deep end with C/C++, which I haven't used much since then, but can make some sense of). I started out after high school as a COBOL(!) developer and spent a couple of years supporting and exxtending accounting and payroll systems, including doing patches related to the Millenium (or Y2K) bug.
- **Systems Infrastructure:** - I subsequently joined university, developed an interest in networking, systems and infrastructure (when not attending EEE lectures), and spent time hanging out with the early Linux and Open Source community. Post university found myself in the telco space doing OSS deployments and supports across several telcos. The telco space was booming at the time.
- **Strategy and Ops:** - I've often worn a business operations + strategy hat, I did this while running the iHub (this was a huge learning experience, and was fun + stress combined), and while attempting to build a couple of companies.
- **Cloud + DevOps (+ some development with Golang):** I started exploring cloud in depth in 2018/2019, while we were building a prototype SME  financing platform on Google Cloud.
- **Data Engineering, Data Science:** - I've always been interested in data and makign sense of it. I did the 16 week course at [WQU's Applied Data Science Lab](https://www.wqu.edu/). For this, I'm now working on personal projects, and will be highlighting some here.

Beyond Cloud (Infra, DevOps) and Data, I've had an interest in infosec and blockchain tech. For one of our projects in 2018, we looked at the Stellar Blockchain as a possible platform.

> I decided to focus my efforts on Data and Cloud.

I'll do this while keeping tabs in general with infosec, blockchain and web3. I suspect that in order to really go deep on infosec I may have to sign up to a somewhat more structured or formal means of learning.

I'm focusing on Data and Cloud simply because collectively they bring my interests and experiences together, and there are lots of new things to learn and new complex problems to solve.
