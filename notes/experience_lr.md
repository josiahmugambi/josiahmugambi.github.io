## Experience (and Linear Regression)

Someone suggested to me recently that experience is knowing what *not* to do in a given scenario.

Here is an analogy as I refresh on machine learning:

*Over-fitting*: the illusion of having all the experience one needs resulting one naively thinking that they have a perfect solution to a problem.

*Under-fitting*: not enough experience to provide a good enough solution.

Gaining experience in a particular area takes time and practice.

A second analogy from [Linear Regression](https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/blog/2020/10/linear-regression-for-absolute-beginners-with-implementation-in-python/) & Gradient Descent:

"A learning rate that is too large can cause the model to converge too quickly to a suboptimal solution, whereas a learning rate that is too small can cause the process to get stuck"

Too small a learning rate? Likely means takes one too long to get the right level of experience, probably because of not learning enough.

Too large a learning rate?  i.e. too much information is not always a good thing, jumping from one thing to another.

For the ML gurus, does this make sense?
