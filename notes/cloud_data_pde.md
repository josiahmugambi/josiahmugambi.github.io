## Attempting the Google Cloud Professional Data Engineering Certification Exam

TL;DR: Keep learning and stay curious. The journey and real life experience matter more.

"Competence over credentials" -James Clear

--
I would describe my motivations when attempting this certification test as akin to checking the fuel gauge while on a journey and noting where and when you need to stop and top up. It was also a forcing function to ramp up learning on a few topics.

The Professional Data Engineer is apparently one the hardest of the Google Cloud certification tests, probably due to its breadth, and knowledge and experience required. One needs an understanding of big data - knowing a bit about Apache's #bigdata ecosystem helps - as well as architecting, programming, SQL, data storage, machine learning, and of course familiarity with Google Cloud Data and AI platforms, cloud infrastructure knowledge (compute, storage, networking, security) and best practices. Experience matters greatly.

From a Google Cloud certification point of view, it felt like the Professional Cloud Architect prep on steroids. It also felt like it would take years of practical experience to really know *all* of this stuff.

This brings me to a key lesson: getting a certification shouldn't be seen as a sign that one knows *everything* in that domain or topic. In my view, it is a check point serving to affirm that one has some usable knowledge. There are always gaps in plenty, especially as change in technology is constant. This is probably a major reason why for instance AWS exams are valid for only 3 years and Google Cloud exams for 2 years, with many others following a similar cadence.

Failing an exam also doesn't mean one doesn't know anything. Once upon a time, getting a Cisco certification was the rage, yet I knew of highly qualified and experienced engineers who never attained a single certification, but were highly respected and sought after. The goal of learning shouldn't be to sit and pass an exam. The certifications rather are like sign posts on the road or that fuel gauge in the car, and not the destination. At the end of the day, experience and competence are what matter.

~~

The exam:

Big Query (BQ), along with DataFlow, Data Proc featured extensively. I didn't get round to counting, but I'd imagine ~60% of the questions featured BQ in one form or the other. There were lots of pub/sub + dataflow, dataproc + apache big data ecosystem related questions. Also present were numerous migration scenarios (e.g. Apache Hadoop to dataproc) and one hybrid one. One also needed to understand the Machine Learning models available on Google Cloud. I would imagine the Machine Learning certification gets into a lot more depth.

There is significant overlaps with the Professional Cloud Architect exam (particularly on storage and compute), but that is no surprise as the PCA covers the entire breadth of Google Cloud.

I did 3 Udemy prep exams which helped along with the sample exam (which is about 29 questions) as well doing the data engineering quest on [Cloud Skills Boost](https://www.cloudskillsboost.google/quests/25)

It certainly was not easy, but I learnt quite a bit while studying, and it has given me impetus and lots of new areas to delve into.
